Tartarus

Most habit trackers give you in‑app rewards that feel like Monopoly money, or a streak counter that’s just a number. Neither one really changes whether you show up tomorrow.

This one works more like a game, but without the fake currency.

You set the habits you want to track. Each time you complete one, you deal damage to a boss—a stand‑in for your own inertia. The boss’s health scales to how many habits you’re tracking, so it stays proportional whether you’re doing three things or twelve.

Miss too many days and you don’t just lose a streak. You go to Tartarus. While you’re there, you stop earning rewards and you have to complete a set of penance tasks—tasks you choose ahead of time—before you can get back to your boss. The idea is simple: if you slip, you pay a real cost, but one you’ve defined yourself.

When you do earn something—by defeating a boss, hitting a streak, or just logging a tough habit—it goes into a reward log. You decide when to claim it. No loot boxes, or useless cosmetics

It’s just a structure. The point is to give your habits stakes and make the cost of skipping something real—without relying on gimmicks.
